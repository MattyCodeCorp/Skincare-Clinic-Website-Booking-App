Product Requirements Document (PRD)Project: Clinic Frontend Website & Booking Module DemoDocument Stage: Conceptual / Client PitchFocus: Frontend Customer Experience, UI/UX Optimization, Integrated Booking1. Sample Branding & Visual Identity (Demo Purposes)To present a cohesive vision, we will use a premium, modern brand identity that moves away from the typical "sterile" hospital look, aiming for an Apple-inspired, high-fidelity aesthetic.Clinic Name: Lumière Skin AestheticsTagline: "The Science of Radiant Skin."Color Palette: Soft neutrals (Warm Ivory, Pearl White, Ash Grey) accented with a signature calming tone (e.g., Soft Sage Green or Muted Rose Gold) to convey luxury and hygiene without feeling cold.Typography: A clean, geometric Sans-Serif (like Inter or SF Pro for UI elements) paired with an elegant Serif (like Playfair Display) for main headings to establish a premium feel.Visual Style: High-resolution photography, soft glassmorphism for UI cards, subtle drop shadows, and cinematic lighting on treatment visuals.2. Problem Statement & UX SolutionsCurrent dermatology and skin clinic websites frequently suffer from poor user experiences that cause high drop-off rates. This project directly addresses these industry pain points.Current Pain Point (The Problem)Proposed Solution (Lumière UX)Clunky, Redirected Booking: Users are forced off-site to outdated third-party portals with terrible mobile optimization.Integrated Seamless Module: A smooth, slide-out side sheet or modal booking flow directly within the site. Zero redirects.Information Overload: Dense blocks of medical jargon that overwhelm patients looking for cosmetic solutions.Scannable UI Cards: Treatments presented with clear, scannable icons, tags (e.g., "Downtime: 2 days", "Pain Level: Low"), and interactive Before/After sliders.Sterile & Intimidating Vibe: Websites look like hospital portals, inducing anxiety rather than excitement.Spa-Like Aesthetic: High-fidelity, beautifully graded visuals with smooth micro-interactions to create a calming, premium digital environment.Ambiguous Pricing/Consultation Steps: Patients are unsure what happens next or what the financial commitment is.Transparent Journey Mapping: A clear visual timeline of the consultation process and transparent starting prices.3. Key Frontend Features & Page Structure3.1. Homepage (The Digital Lobby)Hero Section: Full-width, high-quality video background (subtle, slow-motion skincare routine or clinical precision) with a clear, floating call-to-action (CTA): "Book a Consultation".Quick Service Bar: A horizontal, scrollable sticky bar with quick links to popular concerns (Acne, Anti-Aging, Pigmentation, Laser).Social Proof / Trust Signals: Embedded Google reviews or Trustpilot scores, along with logos of premium skincare brands used in the clinic.Interactive "Target Your Concern" Map: A visual representation of a face/body where users can click areas to see recommended treatments.3.2. Treatments & Services PagesCategorized Grid: Services broken down by "Concerns" and "Treatments" using clean, rounded UI cards with subtle hover states.Detailed Treatment View:Overview Tab: What it is, who it's for.Expectations Tab: Preparation, procedure time, recovery time, and aftercare (using easy-to-read UI chips).Interactive Gallery: High-quality Before/After image sliders (draggable).Contextual CTA: A "Book this Treatment" button that auto-populates the booking module with the selected service.3.3. The Seamless Booking Module (Core Feature)Trigger: Accessible from a sticky header button or contextual buttons on service pages.UI Pattern: A multi-step right-side slide-out drawer (prevents the user from losing their place on the main website).Flow:Select Intent: New Patient vs. Returning Patient.Select Service: Searchable dropdown with popular treatments pinned at the top. (Option to select "General Consultation" if unsure).Select Specialist (Optional): UI cards showing doctor profiles with their specialties.Calendar Picker: A modern, mobile-friendly date and time picker. Only shows available slots.Details & Confirmation: Minimal form (Name, Phone, Email, SMS OTP verification for spam reduction).Success State: Delightful micro-animation confirming the booking, with quick buttons to "Add to Google Calendar" or "Add to Apple Calendar".4. UI/UX Design Principles & RequirementsMobile-First Architecture: Over 70% of clinic traffic will be mobile. Touch targets must be at least 44x44px. Bottom-sheet menus should be used for mobile navigation instead of hard-to-reach hamburger menus.Micro-Interactions: Implement subtle loading skeletons instead of spinning wheels. Use soft fade-ins for elements as the user scrolls down the page (scroll-triggered animations).Accessibility (a11y): Ensure WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. High contrast ratios for text, ARIA labels for the booking module calendar, and full keyboard navigability.Performance: The UI must feel instantaneous. Images should be lazy-loaded and served in next-gen formats (WebP/AVIF).5. Next Steps for PitchingWireframing: Create low-fidelity structural maps of the Homepage and Booking Module flow.High-Fidelity Prototyping: Design 2-3 key screens in Figma (Homepage, Treatment Detail, and Booking Modal) using the "Lumière" branding to demonstrate the premium feel.Client Review: Present the prototype to gather feedback on the visual direction and user flow before discussing backend integrations (CRMs, EMRs, Payment Gateways).
